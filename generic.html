Smoking is expensive. In fact, the amount of money you spend on smoking may surprise you. •Multiply how much money you spend on tobacco every day by 365 to see what you spend every year on smoking. •Now multiply that by the number of years you have been using tobacco. •Then, multiply the cost per year by 10 for the upcoming 10 years. If you’re a one-pack-per-day smoker, you’ll probably save over $15,000. Ask yourself what you would rather do with that much money! There are other financial benefits, too. You’ll pay less for health and life insurance. You’ll incur fewer costs due to tobacco-related problems, medical bills, and frequent trips to the doctor. If you quit smoking, you will also decrease the chance of fatal fires and serious burns. And because you aren’t making late-night trips to the store for cigarettes, you’ll gain more freedom and time – the cost of which can be immeasurable.
